TIMEOUT_CYCLES = BAUD_DIVISOR * 11 * 20
timeoutError should assert when: byteWaitCounter == TIMEOUT_CYCLES - 1 
- (= BAUD_DIVISOR * 11 * 20 - 1)
- byteWaitCounter increments every clock cycle (when waiting)

The cycle after a byteReadyEdge is detected, which is instantly (see definition), the FSM
switches to a new state and this state starts counting byteWaitCounter.

In the test:
- send_byte(SENTINEL)
- UART asserts byteReady (on cycle ~6 of hold_for_bit_period of send_byte, 
see messageReady_timing.txt)
- cycle after: message_rx checks SENTINEL (cycle 7 of hold_for_bit_period of send_byte)
- cycle after: message_rx is in MSG_TYPE state, waiting for next byte (cycle 8 of 
hold_for_bit_period of send_byte). Here byteWaitCounter is 0, and +1 for every cycle after
- The test pauses for exactly TIMEOUT_CYCLES clock cycles (from cycle '9' of hold_for_bit_period)
- At the end byteWaitCounter should be TIMEOUT_CYCLES, so that means the timeoutError
has already asserted the cycle before

=> this test definition should trigger a timeout warning

NOTE: this is actually a worst-case lower bound, as byteWaitCounter might start incrementing
on an earlier clock cycle, s.t. there are more clock cycles of slack than just 1 (for triggering
the timeout error).
